About Me

I'm a dedicated high school student at Athens High School in Troy, Michigan, with a passion for scientific research, particularly in computational biology and medical sciences. As a National Merit Semifinalist and AP Scholar with Distinction, I've consistently pursued academic excellence while engaging in meaningful research and leadership roles.

My research spans from neuronal vesicle formation in Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease to innovative solutions for water quality testing and sustainable lubricants. Beyond academics, I serve as President of Science Olympiad, lead community service initiatives, and contribute to healthcare through volunteering at Corewell Health.

I'm committed to bridging scientific inquiry with real-world impact, whether through research publications, leadership in student organizations, or service to my community.

Homemade Oxidation Reduction Probe

Award Winner

SEFMD 2025 - Great Lakes Water Authority Award (2nd Place)

Created a cheaper Oxidation Reduction Probe using commonly available items to measure quantities of different ionic compounds in water.

  • 20% of the cost with equal/improved performance compared to industry standard
  • Presented findings at Science and Engineering Fair of Metro Detroit 2025
  • Won 2nd place prize with Great Lakes Water Authority

DIY Tribometer for Sustainable Lubricant Oil
